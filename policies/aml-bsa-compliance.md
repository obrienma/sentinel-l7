# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) Compliance Policy

**Policy ID:** AML-BSA-001  
**Effective Date:** 2025-01-01  
**Scope:** All financial transactions processed through the Sentinel-L7 compliance monitoring platform.

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the obligations of the organization under the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), the USA PATRIOT Act, and applicable anti-money laundering (AML) regulations enforced by the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN). The goal is to detect, deter, and report suspected money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes.

---

## 2. Currency Transaction Reports (CTR)

Financial institutions must file a Currency Transaction Report (CTR) with FinCEN for any cash transaction—or series of related cash transactions—exceeding $10,000 in a single business day. This requirement applies to deposits, withdrawals, exchanges, or payments made in currency.

Structuring transactions specifically to avoid the $10,000 CTR threshold—known as "structuring"—is itself a federal crime under 31 U.S.C. § 5324, regardless of whether the underlying funds are from a legitimate source.

Indicators of structuring include: multiple cash deposits just below $10,000 within a short timeframe, transactions split across multiple branches or accounts, and patterns suggesting deliberate avoidance of reporting thresholds.

---

## 3. Suspicious Activity Reports (SAR)

A Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) must be filed with FinCEN when a financial institution knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect that a transaction involves funds derived from illegal activity, is intended to evade reporting requirements, lacks a lawful purpose, or involves use of the institution to facilitate criminal activity.

The SAR filing threshold is $5,000 for transactions involving a known suspect and $25,000 when no suspect can be identified.

SARs must be filed within 30 calendar days of the date the suspicious activity was initially detected. A 60-day extension is available when no suspect has been identified at the time of detection.

---

## 4. High-Risk Transaction Categories

The following transaction types carry elevated AML risk and require enhanced due diligence:

Cash-intensive businesses such as casinos, car dealerships, check cashers, and jewelry retailers are considered high-risk due to the volume of currency they handle and the ease with which illicit funds can be commingled with legitimate revenue.

Cross-border wire transfers, particularly to or from jurisdictions identified as high-risk by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF), require additional scrutiny. Transactions involving FATF blacklisted or grey-listed jurisdictions must be reviewed against current sanctions lists.

Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)—individuals who hold or have held prominent public positions—pose elevated corruption and bribery risk. All transactions involving PEPs or their immediate family members require enhanced due diligence.

---

## 5. Anomaly Score Thresholds

Transactions flagged by automated monitoring systems with an anomaly score above 0.80 must be reviewed by a compliance officer within 24 hours. Transactions with an anomaly score above 0.90 require immediate escalation and must not be released for settlement until cleared.

Automated systems must not be relied upon exclusively—human review is required for any transaction that meets SAR filing thresholds regardless of the system's anomaly score.

---

## 6. Record Retention

Financial institutions must retain all BSA-related records—including CTRs, SARs, and supporting documentation—for a minimum of five years from the date of the report or the date of the transaction, whichever is later.

Records must be retrievable within a reasonable time upon request from FinCEN, federal banking regulators, or law enforcement. Electronic records are acceptable provided they can be reproduced in a legible format.

---

## 7. Sanctions Compliance

All transactions must be screened against the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list prior to processing. Transactions involving a designated person or entity must be blocked or rejected, and a report must be filed with OFAC within ten business days.

The organization must also screen against the EU Consolidated Sanctions List and the UN Security Council Consolidated List for cross-border transactions.

---

## 8. Enforcement and Penalties

Willful violations of BSA requirements can result in civil penalties of up to $25,000 per violation per day, criminal fines up to $500,000, and imprisonment of up to ten years for individuals. Institutions may also face enhanced regulatory scrutiny, consent orders, and reputational damage.

Failure to implement an adequate AML compliance program—including written policies, a designated compliance officer, ongoing training, and independent testing—constitutes a separate violation independent of any underlying transaction violations.
